I. General Information

1. Book

Lexile: 800L
Audio time: 24 minutes
Pages: 32
The school day of colonial children in New England, the Middle Colonies, and the Southern Colonies is examined. The usual schoolteacher is described, as well as urban and rural schoolhouses. There's even an exercise to help today's reader connect with the past.

2. Author

Shelley Swanson Sateren graduated with an English major from Augsburg College, in Minneapolis. Shelley worked as a bookseller at the Red Balloon Bookshop for five years and as a non-fiction children's book editor at Dillon/Macmillan for two years. For the past seven years, she has been a professional nanny, caring for children aged 0-6. She has read countless board books and picture books to the kids, as well as numerous chapter books. She taught them many literacy skills too.

II. Main Idea

This book talks about how it’s going in the school in Colonial America. Here, students ranged in age from 4 to 20 years old, when their parents needed them to work at home, they did not go to school. Many students did chores before school, went to school from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m., did more chores, and then played afterward. Education nowadays is way more different than back in time, poorer students usually skipped school to enter an apprenticeship while rich students had private tutoring and others attended dame schools. However, in colonial times, most people did not believe girls needed further education. Girls learned enough reading, writing, and arithmetic to be able to study the Bible and manage family finances. Many students at that time got depression and anxiety attacked by their school punishments, they were punished harshly. They got whipped or they were hit by a switch. If they forgot their lessons, they had to sit in the corner with a dunce cap on their head. This and every other book in the series “It's Back to School ... Way Back!” describes some aspect of colonial school life. This certainly wasn't riveting information, but for a young reader, this would be the lesson.

IV. Glossary

Indentured servants: Indentured servants were men and women who signed a contract by which they agreed to work for a certain number of years in exchange for transportation
Missionary: Person who works on behalf of a religious group to spread the group’s faith
Apprentice: a person who works for learns from a skilled professional for a set amount of time
Recitation: the action of repeating something aloud from memory
Devotion: a prayer, worship, other religious activity
Psalm: religious song or poem
Penmanship: The art of writing by hand
Catechism: a collection of questions and answers that are used to teach people about the Christian religion
